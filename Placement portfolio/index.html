Critical Analysis/Reflection: WIL Placement at Stegowl Technologies
Describe (D):
During my Work Integrated Learning (WIL) placement at Stegowl Technologies, I was primarily focused on frontend development, with an emphasis on improving the user interface and user experience (UI/UX) of a Laravel-based furniture e-commerce platform. My tasks included ensuring mobile responsiveness, implementing interactive elements, and collaborating with backend developers to seamlessly integrate frontend functionalities with the backend systems.

I also contributed to multiple ongoing projects, often working alongside full-stack developers to enhance design elements, improve frontend components, and resolve cross-browser compatibility issues.
Interpret (I):
Throughout my placement, I encountered a variety of frontend challenges. One significant challenge occurred when the e-commerce website struggled with mobile responsiveness. Users on smaller devices experienced distorted layouts, which hindered their browsing experience. This made me realize the importance of thoroughly testing across various devices and using responsive design techniques such as flexible grids and media queries.

Moreover, the team environment exposed me to different approaches to frontend development. Initially, I found it challenging to adapt to the diverse coding styles and feedback from senior developers. However, this experience taught me to value feedback and recognize the importance of adopting a flexible mindset when solving UI/UX issues. Working collaboratively also improved my ability to communicate design decisions effectively within a development team.
Evaluate (E):
My prior knowledge of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript proved useful as I was able to implement visually appealing components and ensure smooth user interactions. For example, I added subtle animations and transitions that enhanced the overall user experience. However, my hesitancy to ask for clarification on design expectations early on caused delays in delivering some features. I also found myself spending excessive time on minor UI details, which affected my ability to meet project deadlines.

Another area where I needed improvement was managing multiple projects at once. At times, I struggled to balance the various frontend tasks with the pressure of ongoing development projects. This led to a lack of prioritization, causing me to focus too much on polishing individual components without considering the broader project deadlines.
Plan (P):
To improve, I plan to enhance my understanding of responsive design by exploring modern frameworks such as Tailwind CSS and adopting better design strategies for mobile-first development. I also intend to deepen my knowledge of JavaScript frameworks like React and Vue.js, which will allow me to handle more complex state management and create dynamic web applications that cater to a wide range of user needs.

Additionally, I aim to improve my collaboration skills by being more proactive in seeking feedback and clarification earlier in the development process. By clearly communicating design choices and addressing potential issues early, I can ensure smoother collaboration with team members and more efficient project progression.

To address time management challenges, I will prioritize tasks using tools like Trello and Jira to set clear milestones for each development phase. This structured approach will help me maintain a balance between quality and deadlines while ensuring consistent progress.
Conclusion:
Overall, this reflection has highlighted my strengths in frontend development and areas where I need to grow. Moving forward, I am committed to continuously improving my skills, expanding my knowledge of modern development frameworks, and enhancing my ability to work effectively within development teams. These improvements will allow me to deliver more impactful and efficient solutions as a developer.

Technical & Transferable Skills: WIL Placement at Stegowl Technologies
Artefact 1: Frontend Development Using HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap

Context:
During my WIL placement at Stegowl Technologies, I was assigned the task of designing and developing the frontend of an eCommerce website for a furniture shop. This project required creating user-friendly and visually appealing web pages for product categories, a shopping cart, and an admin panel. The main challenge was ensuring the website’s responsiveness and adaptability across various devices using HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap.

Action:
To achieve this, I structured the pages using HTML and used CSS for custom styling. I utilized Bootstrap’s grid system to ensure responsiveness, allowing the website to maintain a consistent layout across different screen sizes. After understanding the client’s requirements, I designed wireframes and customized pre-built Bootstrap components like navigation bars and buttons to match the furniture shop’s branding.

One key task involved creating a grid format for product listings, making it easier for users to browse and select products. I used media queries to ensure the layout adapted smoothly to mobile devices. To validate my work, I tested the interface on various screen sizes, making adjustments as needed to ensure all elements, such as buttons and images, aligned properly.

Result:
The final product was a responsive, user-friendly interface that improved the website’s usability across devices. The eCommerce site’s performance on mobile devices increased significantly due to the optimized design and layout changes. The result was a polished frontend that contributed positively to the shop’s online presence and improved the customer experience.

Learning:
Through this project, I enhanced my skills in frontend development and responsive design. I became more proficient in using Bootstrap for layout management and in customizing components to meet specific client needs. This experience also strengthened my understanding of UX design principles, particularly in creating visually appealing and functional web pages that cater to different devices. These skills are crucial for my goal of becoming a skilled frontend developer, and they align with my IT major, where web development plays a key role. This project helped me realize the importance of both design aesthetics and performance optimization for web applications.

Artefact 2: Frontend-Admin Panel and Dynamic Components

Context:
In addition to the main eCommerce site, I worked on developing the admin panel for the shop owner to manage product listings and customer orders. While the backend functionality was handled by another team member using Laravel, my responsibility was to create a frontend interface that was intuitive and easy for non-technical users to navigate. My focus was on implementing dynamic elements that allowed for real-time updates without the need for full page reloads, enhancing the overall efficiency of the system.

Action:
I used HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap to design a simple and clean admin dashboard. The Bootstrap modals and form components were customized to make the product management process straightforward, allowing the administrator to easily add, edit, and delete products. To improve interactivity, I used JavaScript and jQuery to incorporate dynamic features such as real-time form validation. I also implemented AJAX calls to update specific sections of the admin panel, providing instant feedback without reloading the entire page.

Result:
The admin panel was functional and user-friendly, allowing the shop owner to manage products and orders efficiently. The dynamic components I implemented helped to streamline the workflow by reducing page reload times and improving responsiveness. The real-time updates provided a smoother user experience, making the admin panel more interactive and productive.

Learning:
This experience gave me valuable insights into dynamic web development and how frontend and backend systems interact. I honed my skills in JavaScript and jQuery, which are critical tools for enhancing interactivity in web applications. Furthermore, this project helped me understand the importance of usability in administrative interfaces, an often-overlooked aspect of web development. These technical skills, combined with my ability to collaborate effectively with backend developers, are essential for my future career aspirations as a full-stack developer, where building dynamic and user-centric applications is a key focus.

Ethical Conduct and Professional Accountability
Context (c):
During my placement at Stegowl Technologies, I encountered a situation that highlighted the importance of ethical conduct in the ICT industry, specifically around data privacy. While working on a customer data collection form for an eCommerce website, the initial draft requested an excessive amount of personal information, including details that were not necessary for the user’s shopping experience. This raised concerns about potential misuse of personal data and data privacy risks.
Action (A):
Recognizing the ethical implications, I advocated for a more responsible approach by suggesting that we only collect the essential data needed for order processing. I recommended revising the form to align with data privacy best practices and suggested adding a clear privacy policy that explained how customer data would be used. Additionally, when the project began integrating AI-driven customer interactions, I emphasized the importance of transparency, ensuring users were informed when they were interacting with an AI rather than a human representative.
Result (R):
My recommendations led to a more ethically sound data collection process. The final design of the form requested only essential customer information, and the addition of the privacy policy increased transparency. These changes ensured that the website followed data privacy laws and helped build trust with customers. However, I realized that providing regular team training on data ethics would have improved our collective approach to these issues.
Learning (L):
This experience reinforced the importance of ethical accountability in technology. It highlighted the delicate balance between collecting data for business needs and respecting users' privacy rights. Moving forward, I am committed to being proactive in addressing ethical dilemmas in my work, whether related to data privacy, AI use, or security concerns. I’ve learned that creating a culture of responsibility within teams is essential to ensure that ethical considerations are integrated into every aspect of ICT development.
